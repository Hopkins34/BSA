“Exploring the Outdoors, Empowering the Future.”

Welcome to Troop 375, where Scouts and families can stay connected with events, resources, and the exciting journey of leadership, adventure, and community service.

News:

Here you can read about the newest and upcoming storys in BSA troop 375.

“Preparing for the Annual Winter Campout”

Troop 375 is getting ready for its much-anticipated annual Winter Campout! This event challenges scouts to put their cold-weather survival skills to the test while enjoying the beauty of the frosty outdoors. Activities will include shelter building, snowshoeing, and cooking hearty meals over an open fire. Scouts are also working together to prepare their gear and learn the importance of staying warm and safe in low temperatures. It’s an adventure that builds both confidence and camaraderie.

“Leadership Skills in Action”

Our Patrol Leaders are stepping up this month, leading troop meetings and planning activities. With guidance from troop leaders, they’re organizing team-building games, skill demonstrations, and event logistics. These leadership roles give scouts a chance to practice decision-making, communication, and responsibility in a supportive environment. Watching young leaders thrive and inspire their peers is one of the most rewarding aspects of our troop’s program.

“Earning the Citizenship in the Community Badge”

This month, several scouts are working on their Citizenship in the Community merit badge by exploring ways to make a difference locally. They’ve visited the town council, interviewed community leaders, and brainstormed service projects that could address local needs. The experience is teaching scouts about civic responsibility while fostering a deeper connection to their community. It’s exciting to see them understand how their efforts can create a ripple effect of positive change.

“Gear Drive for New Scouts”

To ensure every scout has what they need for upcoming adventures, Troop 375 is hosting a gear drive to collect gently used equipment. Families and alumni are encouraged to donate backpacks, sleeping bags, and outdoor essentials. This initiative helps new scouts get started without the barrier of costly equipment while reinforcing the troop’s values of support and inclusivity. The drive has already seen a great turnout, and we’re grateful for the generosity of our community.
